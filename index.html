Baccarat!

Rules

The objective of Baccarat is to bet on the hand that will have the highest value, with 9 being the highest possible value. In Baccarat, cards 2-9 are worth their face value, 10s and face cards (J, Q, K) are worth 0, and Aces are worth 1. If the value of a hand exceeds 9, then the value is adjusted by subtracting 10 from the total.

Players bet on whether the Player hand or the Banker hand will win, or if the result will be a Tie. The winning hand is the one that is closer to 9.

Learn about drawing a third card

A natural hand is when either the Player or the Banker is dealt a total of 8 or 9 from their first two cards. When a natural hand is dealt, the game is over and bets are paid out accordingly.

The third card rule in baccarat is one of the ways that the dealer (or banker) has a slight house edge in the game.

When the Player or the Banker has a total of 8 or 9 from their first two cards, no further cards are drawn. If the Player's total is less than or equal to 5, the Player draws a third card. If the Player does not draw a third card, the Banker draws a third card if their total is less than or equal to 5. If the Player does draw a third card, the Banker draws a third card based on the following rules:

  • If the Banker's total is 2 or less, the Banker draws a third card regardless of the Player's third card.
  • If the Banker's total is 3, the Banker draws a third card unless the Player's third card is an 8.
  • If the Banker's total is 4, the Banker draws a third card if the Player's third card is 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7.
  • If the Banker's total is 5, the Banker draws a third card if the Player's third card is 4, 5, 6, or 7.
  • If the Banker's total is 6, the Banker draws a third card if the Player's third card is 6 or 7.
  • If the Banker's total is 7 or higher, then the Banker does not draw a third card.
